Suffixes

Convention/Grammar

Suffixes: Suffixes are word parts added to the end of a base word to change its meaning or function, like "-less" in "careless" or "-able" in "readable."
The spelling of the base word might/might not change.
Base word spelling does not change:

  • hope + -ful = hopeful

Base word spelling does change:

  • debate + -able = debatable (e changes to a)
